ANIMAL KINGDOM

     The animal kingdom includes different organisms that are characterized by their capacity to move, their cells without cell wall nor chloroplasts, and their embryogenesis (how new life is created and developed). Human beings belong to this kingdom.

ANIMAL CLASSIFICATION

     Animals are living organisms which are heterotrophic and have motility. They are divided in two groups: vertebrates and invertebrates.

  • VERTEBRATES. Vertebrate animals are those who have vertebral column. Within this group, there are 5 families of animals: fish, birds, reptiles, amphibians and mammals. Each family has its own characteristics.
  • INVERTEBRATES. Invertebrates don't have internal skeleton, although some species have hard parts.
