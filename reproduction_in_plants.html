Reproduction in plants

     There are two types of reproduction:

  • Sexual reproduction
  • Asexual reproduction

ASEXUAL REPRODUCTION

     Asexual plants are capable of create a new plant without needing sexual cells, even when they have flowers. Asexual reproduction systems are:

  • Spores reproduction. They are cells that can form a new plant when they fall to the ground.
  • Bulb, tubers, rhizomes, stolons and slips.

 

SEXUAL REPRODUCTION

     Plants' sexual reproduction takes place through flowers, which are where the reproductive organs of this plants are. Sexual reproduction involves two types of cells: female and male. From the union of them, a new plant comes out.
