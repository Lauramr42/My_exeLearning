Invertebrates

     Invertebrates don't have internal skeleton: inside them there are no bones, and they don't have backbone. They have soft inner bodies which are held in shape by a flexible covering of outer cells.

     However, some invertebrate species have got some hard parts, such as shells or conches, to protect them. Others have a rigid covering called exoskeleton. Finally, some of them have got a soft body without protection.
