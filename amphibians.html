Amphibians

     Amphibians have the following characteristics:

  • Thin and humid skin, without fur, scales or feathers. Their skin is prepared to protect the animal. Their skin protects, controls their corporal temperature and it helps breathing. It can also produce toxic substances that persuades enemies.
  • Amphibians transform themselves in their growth, passing from living in the water, to living outside it. They breathe through gills (they live in the water), through lungs (they live both in humid areas and on the ground), and through their skin.
  • They reproduce through eggs.
  • They can't control their body temperature, which is called cold-blooded (exothermic).
